21:07TECHNO

This ain't easy. First of all unlike BlitzMax, C++ has no garbage collector, but as most of the stuff was written in a way that does easily allow me get aroud that, it should be no true issue, but don't be fooled, as one mistake on my hand here and memory leaks are inevitable and never to be seen unless unexplainable "Out Of Memory" issues occur in game, and finding out where I went wrong will be very extremely nasty.

The audio department in particular can cause me to suffer here. Of course, I can make sure of uniquepointers and sharedpointers, although I do need to do some research on them before I fully use those, this to make absolutely sure they cannot doom me. I've met my demise more more trivial things when it comes to C and C++ to be honest especially where OOP is concerned and unfortunately the LAURA II engine is ENTIRELY based on OOP. Well... er... OOPs.... to make a bad pun.
Now I have once converted an engine of mine manually from Visual Basic to BlitzMax, however that was not even half the trouble as I may be experiencing now. Visual Basic and BlitzMax do at least share a lot when it comes to memory management, or at least enough to to put the worry to me...

Ultimately the bottom line is anything BlitzMax can do C++ can do as well, and I know that I cannot accomplish that without editing one single line of Lua code (particularly in the start up and in the checkups for Anna's Temple I will need to adapt or even re-write a few scripts, I am certain of that), but at least I am sure I can get quite far, and if anyone can do this... I can....

Sounds arrogant, but if that is not the spirit you have when undertaking an ordeal such as this, your project is cursed from the start.
