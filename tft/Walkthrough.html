Welcome
Hello folks, welcome to this official walkthrough.
Of course, I think walkthroughs suck, but if you ever get stuck in this game, by all means, use this walkthrough to get back on track.
Now I should note that in lots of puzzle the solution is randomly generated, meaning that I cannot know the exact outcome of the puzzle, as it's different each playthrough, however, I can help you to find out how to think to solve the puzzle.
Now in most dungeons all rooms are numbered with a "#xxx" format. I will use this format in this walkthrough too.
If you don't know what room you are in just don't move and the game will show you a few quick stats telling you.
Each room I'll tell you the room number (if one is available) and give you point wise directions (if possible).
Like this
#001:>=Go East
=Go North
=Pull the switch
=Hit the door North
#002:>=Go West
=Exit South
"Exit" means in my work that you leave the room you are in, and I'll give the direction where to exit the room.
Also all dungeons and bosses are indexed, so you can easily look things up.
At the start of every dungeon or boss fight, I will show you the portraits of the characters in my party, plus their respective levels at the time. Please note these were MY levels, and not recommended levels.
Also note that this game has a flexible level cap. The level cap starts at 5 in the easy mode 4 in the normal mode and 3 in the hard mode, and it raises with the same numbers each time you find a traveler's emblem.
Due to this my levels can technically speaking be higher than your level cap, since I know where to find all emblems.
Well let's get this show on the road. ;)

Cast of characters
Your main cast is Jake, Marrilona, Dandor and Hando Stillor. Except for a small portion of the game were a guest character makes his/her appearance you play the entire game with these four, and people won't leave the party, so that makes things easy, huh?
BioCombat
Jake
Jake is a young warrior born and raised in Vandar

Jake has a friendly and honest personality and always wants to help those in trouble even at the cost of his own life if he has to.

Jake will fall in love with Marrilona of the Fairy Tribe. Sworn enemies to the humans.

In combat Jake is a master with the sword. He is pretty strong, agile and versatile.

He's basically the most balanced character in the game

Marrilona
Marrilona is a young female Fairy born in raised in the village of Frendor.
She has a very cheerful nature, but is also a bit naive and too curious for her own good.

She's known for getting herself in trouble all the time, and easily cries when she is confronted with the results of her own mischief.

However Marrilona is very honest and does have a sense of responsibility and she always accepts the blame for her own trouble and is always eager to set things right

Marrilona excels in the use of magic. Although she does have a few healing and assistance spells, her true power lies in offensive spells. If you know where the enemies weaknesses lie, she can be a natural born killer.

Marrilona's uses wands as her weapon and her wands all have their own spell Marrilona can use for free in stead of doing a physical attack as long as she has the specific wand equipped.

Physically Marrilona is the weakest character (which is a natural trait for her race).

If Marrilona is on low-health she can cast all her spells for free

Dandor
Dandor is a Dwarf from Grombo. Like all Dwarves Dandor is a strong guy an will protect anybody physically weaker than himself (much to Marrilona's annoyance).

Dandor hates cowards and makes no attempts to hide it, and although he speaks in an odd grammar, he is not stupid, and has a strong sense of duty and justice

Let's not beat around the bush, Dandor is physically the strongest guy around, both in offense and defense. However he is very slow and not very accurate. He can take a blow or two, but his magic defense is not very high.
Hando Stillor
Hando Stillor is an Elfish High Priest in the Order of Weniaria, the Goddess of Healing.

He is a man of justice and wisdom, and he acts as the party's mentor.

His biggest desire is to end the racial war.

Since he is high priest for a healing goddess I guess it's pretty obvious that Hando Stillor is your main healer.

He can learn the strongest healing spells and you will need his powers bad.

When one of his allies is KO'ed, Hando Stillor might instantly revive him or her and put him or her on full HP. However, the more often he does this over the course of the game, the smaller the chance will be this ability is triggered, so... don't get KO'ed too often counting on him to revive you... ok?

Prologue

After Hando Stillor concluded his introduction speech you can choose a character to start the game with.
It basically doesn't matter who you pick since after you did his/her introduction chapter the game will come back to this screen and you will have to pick another character, and the game keeps on doing this until you have them all.
I don't really care in which order you take them on, but I guess it's only obvious that I will just go in the same order as the game shows them. In order words, Jake, Marrilona, Dandor an Hando Stillor.
Well, talked enough already.
Let's go!

Chapter 1 - How they met

You start chapter 1 with either Jake or Marrilona, and you will very soon meet a boss and meet the other in the process...
During this chapter you'll only be playing with these two. Hando Stillor and Dandor won't join the group prior to chapter 2 (which also means that if you in the demo, you will not meet them at all any more).
Also, if you are playing casual mode killing 10 enemies of the same kind will lead the next one to go "oversoul" making it extra strong. In the hard mode this will even happen every 5 kills.
In the easy mode this doesn't happen at all.
Bosses and enemies who could also be encountered in the prologue will never go oversoul.

Masters
There are several people out there called masters.
If you fit the requirement they've set up, you can apprentice under them.
Some stats will be raised or lowered for as long as you apprentice under a master.
While you apprentice they can set you some extra challenges and when you complete them they might teach you some new skills.
There is no need to get into the deep what masters can teach you, since all information is provided by the game itself.
This list will only tell you where to find the masters and what they want from you.
*:>Rubine
=Location: Outside Jake's hut
=Type: Rogue
=Requirement: None
Rubine can teach you very handy skills. She wants you to find money. The amount of times you find money scores you points. It does not matter if you find only 1 shilder or 5000 shilders. You just score a point each time you find shilders either dropped by enemies, found in treasure chests or treasure bags. Selling stuff for money does NOT count. Among the moves she can teach you are pickpocket and a few nasty moves the enemies won't like.
*:>Freya
=Location: Elven Road
=Type: Healer
=Requirement: Give her all your salves and potions
Freya is a very complex master in her ways of teaching, and it for that reason you don't see any points in her status screen. For Jake and Marrilona their water magic counts most and for Hando Stillor his holy skills with Weniaria. Dandor cannot learn any spells from here. She does teach VERY valuable healing spells, but while apprenticing under her your accuracy goes down, so don't push it.
NOTE! If you play the demo you can only find Rubine and Freya. All the other masters can only be found when you got the full version
*:>Midas
=Location: Iskarderiu
=Type: Thief
=Requirement: All your money
Midas can teach you moves that will help you increase your money faster, however he wants you to gain a lot of money in order to learn a new move.
*:>Fandalora
=Location: Frendor
=Type: Magician
=Requirement: A certain average of your skill levels.
Fandalora can teach you magic of the dark kind, like how to curse your enemies, but also how to remove these curses. Dandor cannot learn spells from him.
*:>Russell
=Location: Doubline Bay Forest
=Type: "Pirate"
=Requirement: Beat him in battle
You must beat Russell in order to finish the game, making him the only master who is impossible to miss (although you can always go back to recruit the other masters). He teaches you skills based on your kills and his moves are also in that direction.
*:>Krandar
=Location: Nostraburg
=Type: Warrior
=Requirement: Defeat at least one sealed boss
Krandar can teach you very powerful moves based on how many sealed bosses you beat.
*:>Feenalaria
=Location: Nostraburg
=Type: Sorceress
=Requirement: Have all the other masters
Feenalaria has the most powerful spells of the game, although they are very demanding to use, not to mention it's pretty hard to get her to teach you anything since she cannot be recruited as a master prior to chapter 4. Feenalaria counts the number of victories in battle while apprenticing under her. Dandor cannot learn from her.

Traveler's Emblems
These emblems are VERY important. Not only will they allow you to turn off encounters in the dungeons you find them, they will also raise your level cap by 5 levels in the easy mode, 4 levels in the normal mode and 3 in the hard mode. Except for the prologue dungeons and disappearing dungeons, all dungeons have one. In some dungeons they are lying out in the open, in others they can be hidden in secret passages. This is a quick overview where to find them all.
Main Quest
Frendor Bushes:Close to the North-East exit. Sorry, if you began here as Marrilona you can't get it even though she's close to it, you'll have to wander through this dungeon after Jake and Marrilona met each other
Holy Path:You can see it halfway. It's pretty hard to miss
Road To Hell:Area #003 in the Western sub section
Forbidden Library:Area #005 secret passage
Sidequests
Subterranean River:Area #008 in a secret passage
New Game+ Quests
