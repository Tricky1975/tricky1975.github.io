21:15:59OFFTOPIC

In Memoriam

The man in this video is Chriet Titulaer. In the 1980s he was THE icon in science and most of all technology. His show "Wondere Wereld" (means "Wonder World") was THE show in the Netherlands to invite the normal people to the world of technology. Chriet did in the Netherlands the most important moves to make science popular. He did not only show the newest technology, but he also made many predictions about the technology of the future. Many of his predictions were completely wrong, but there are also A LOT of predictions that came true of of which he became extremely close to the truth (even something he showed (not in this video) as an April Fools joke was actually closer to the truth than anybody could imagine).

Even if you don't speak Dutch, you may want to want this video (if you have a question about what he said about certain items) just ask me, and I'll make a short English version of his story, but many items, speak for themselves.
Also, you may not hear it if you don't understand Dutch, but he speaks with a very heavy accent that is spoken in the South-East part of the Netherlands. He never even tried to hide it, and his looks were very unusual in the Netherlands. Perhaps the fact he was willing to be "out of the mainstream" contributed to his popularity.

Yesterday he passed away at the age of 73 and with him another part of my childhood, but also an icon for Dutch science.

Well, when it comes to Star Story.... I must be very harsh on this now. If your screen is too small for the launcher.... Buy a bigger one. And that goes most of all for Windows users.

I've tried to adapt the launcher to make it compatible for smaller screens, but that only resulted into Windows refusing to draw any of the Window's content.

How do I know this is a bug in Windows itself?

Easy, two reasons make that perfectly clear.
a) On Mac this works PERFECTLY. and b) Dragging the window beyond the edge and dragging it back misshapes it. That only happens when Windows does see a Window as existing, but does not perform any graphic action on it anymore at all.

Well there's more evidence, but these are the two most important reasons.

Now in theory it should be possible to adapt the Launcher The Fairy Tale uses on Star Story as well, but before I get onto that, if first want to know if that launcher does indeed work on smaller monitors.

Since that launcher auto-adapts to the sizes of your screen automatically it should, but when you know MicroSoft as well as I do, you know that what should rarely actually happens.
