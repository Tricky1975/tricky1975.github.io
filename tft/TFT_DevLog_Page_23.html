Due to her love bond with Jake, she is now able to have intercouse,however only after the man she has formed the love bond with (in Marrilona's case that's Jake) robs her of her viriginity. After that she is free to have sex with Tai, however only Jake can make Marrilona pregnant.
Fairies bind themselves to one person of the opposite sex, but they never have sex with that person alone, and despite Jake's jealous personality, he will have to accept this from Marrilona (both Hando Stillor and Krandar try to point this out to him).
The reason why Marrilona refuses to tell Jake straight away what she promised is because she's aware on the taboo on sex and most of all on polygamy among the human culture.
Jake will in turn also have to accept he'll have to have sex with more (Fairy) women than just Marrilona.

Now irony has it that when it comes to equality between the both sexes, the Fairies are on top of the list. Males and females have the same rights and duties and paying a woman less than a man for the same job is something no Fairy would even consider. Fairies love to flirt and to have sex and that is where the differences are made, and they accept the differences Mother Nature brought to the two sexes, but that's where it ends.

The reason why children only carry the name of their father in the "son of" and "daughter of" system is a leftover from ancient times. As the game will explain the origin in the Fairies you may be able to riddle it out for yourself how this came to be.
